The "Academic Misfit" Who Landed 3 Faculty Appointments

Christine E. Ohenewah

Meet Christine

Christine E. Ohenewah is a Cornell-educated attorney and former Big Law litigator who pivoted into academia. She isn't your traditional "Ivory Tower" academic. She brings a dynamic, interdisciplinary energy to the classroom—using Hip Hop lyrics (from "Ye" to "Weezy") to deconstruct complex legal theories like mens rea and self-defense.

Despite her professional pedigree, she felt like an outsider. As a JD holder without a PhD, she was navigating a system designed for a different kind of candidate, unsure if she would ever be viewed as a "real" professor.

The Problem

"I feel like a misfit. Is the PhD a driver's license I don't have?"

Christine was stuck in the "Adjunct Trap." She had the talent and the student reviews, but she was hitting the "PhD Ceiling." Colleagues and mentors told her that without a doctorate, she was hitting a dead end.

She was "shooting in the dark," applying to roles without a clear strategy, and facing the silent rejection of the academic bureaucracy. She had recently been "strung along" for a month by a hiring committee at SUNY Purchase, only to be rejected without feedback.

She came to Professor Town asking a single, burning question: "Is it even possible for me to get a faculty job, or am I wasting my time?"

How Professor Town Helped

We stopped guessing and audited the "Scorecard."

We moved Christine from "anxious applicant" to "strategic operator" by decoding exactly how hiring committees viewed her unique profile.

1. The "Insider" Rubric Audit

We stripped away the imposter syndrome and scored her CV against a real R1 Faculty Hiring Rubric.

The Insight: We demonstrated that she didn't need to apologize for her lack of a PhD. Her "Service" score (launching the Elizabeth Tweneboah Foundation) and "Teaching Excellence" were high-value assets that outweighed the doctoral requirement for her specific target market.

2. The "Portfolio Professor" Strategy

Instead of trying to force herself into a traditional R1 research box, we leaned into her brand as a "Scholar-Practitioner." We targeted institutions that value professional authority (her Big Law background) over theoretical research.

3. The Interview Strategy

We prepped her to stop hiding her "Millennial Energy" and start selling it as her superpower—positioning her as the bridge between the university and the modern student body.

The Results

From "Rejection Fatigue" to 3 Concurrent Faculty Appointments.

Christine didn't just land a job; she built an academic empire. By applying the Professor Town protocols, she secured faculty positions at three distinct institutions, cementing her status as a Professor of Law, Criminology, and Sociology.
