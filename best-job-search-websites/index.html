Global Faculty Recruitment Sources: Comprehensive Directory

This directory organizes the global academic job market into high-yield channels for Professor Town clients. It categorizes platforms by region for generalist searches and by discipline for niche, high-prestige targeting.

Part 1: By Country and Region

United States

The Chronicle of Higher Education (ChronicleVitae)

The primary source for tenure-track and prestige roles at R1 universities and liberal arts colleges. Listings here signal institutional wealth and stable governance.

Website: jobs.chronicle.com

HigherEdJobs

The highest volume board, essential for finding adjunct, remote, and community college positions. It allows deep filtering by institution type and specific sub-disciplines.

Website: higheredjobs.com

Inside Higher Ed Careers

A key competitor to the Chronicle with a strong focus on Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) roles and administrative leadership.

Website: careers.insidehighered.com

Higher Education Recruitment Consortium (HERC)

A non-profit consortium specifically designed to solve the "two-body problem" (dual-career couples) and promote inclusive hiring.

Website: hercjobs.org

AcademicKeys

Targeted heavily toward senior faculty and administrative roles (Deans, Chairs), particularly in STEM and professional fields.

Website: academickeys.com

Canada

University Affairs / Affaires universitaires

The official job board of Universities Canada and the definitive source for tenure-track faculty jobs across the country. It is the "single source of truth" for the Canadian market.

Website: universityaffairs.ca/search-jobs

Academica Careers

Offers broader coverage that includes colleges, polytechnics, and administrative leadership roles, distinct from the research-heavy University Affairs.

Website: academicacareers.ca

CAUT Bulletin (Canadian Association of University Teachers)

Lists positions at unionized institutions, useful for candidates prioritizing strong collective bargaining agreements and labor protections.

Website: caut.ca

United Kingdom

Jobs.ac.uk

The centralized standard for the UK. Virtually every academic job in the UK (from Oxford to post-1992 universities) is listed here. It is the most efficient national board globally.

Website: jobs.ac.uk

Times Higher Education (THE) UniJobs

A global platform with a UK focus. It is particularly strong for senior leadership roles and international universities recruiting UK talent.

Website: timeshighereducation.com/unijobs

Europe (Continental)

Euraxess

The European Commission's official portal for researcher mobility. It is essential for EU-funded research posts (ERC, Marie Curie) and visiting fellowships across all 43+ European countries.

Website: euraxess.ec.europa.eu/jobs

AcademicTransfer (The Netherlands)

The cooperative board of all Dutch research universities. If a job exists at a Dutch research university, it is listed here. It includes tools to match CVs to Dutch academic ranks.

Website: academictransfer.com

Academics.com / Academics.de (Germany/DACH)

The leading board for the German-speaking world (Germany, Austria, Switzerland). It is the primary source for "Chair" (Lehrstuhl) positions and research roles.

Website: academics.com

Academic Positions

A Northern European aggregator that covers Scandinavia (Sweden, Denmark, Norway) and the Benelux region strongly.

Website: academicpositions.com

Jobbnorge (Norway)

The central portal for all Norwegian public sector jobs, including university faculty and research institute positions.

Website: jobbnorge.no

Australia & New Zealand

UniJobs.com.au

The central academic job board for Australian higher education, covering the "Group of Eight" research universities and regional institutions.

Website: unijobs.com.au

Seek (Education Section)

Unlike in the US, this generalist commercial board is heavily used by Australian and NZ universities for vocational (TAFE) and contract academic roles.

Website: seek.com.au / seek.co.nz

Times Higher Education (Australia/NZ Filter)

Frequently used by Australian institutions to recruit international faculty from the UK and US markets.

Website: timeshighereducation.com/unijobs/listings/australia/

Middle East

The Chronicle of Higher Education (Middle East Filter)

The primary recruitment channel for "American-style" universities in the Gulf (e.g., NYU Abu Dhabi, American University of Sharjah, Georgetown Qatar).

Website: jobs.chronicle.com

Akhtaboot

A regional job board that often lists positions for national universities in Jordan, UAE, and Saudi Arabia, though less focused on tenure-track roles than western boards.

Website: akhtaboot.com

Asia (Singapore & Hong Kong)

THE UniJobs (Asia Filter)

The main aggregator for English-speaking universities in Hong Kong (HKU, HKUST) and Singapore (NUS, NTU) seeking global faculty.

Website: timeshighereducation.com/unijobs/listings/asia/

HKU Careers / Careers@SMU

Direct university portals are critical in this region as high-volume research roles (Postdocs/Research Assistant Professors) are sometimes only listed locally.

Website: jobs.hku.hk

Part 2: By Discipline

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math)

Science Careers (AAAS)

The global standard for high-level biomedical, life science, and physical science research positions, including PI roles and prestigious postdocs.

Website: jobs.sciencecareers.org

Nature Careers

Similar to Science Careers but with a stronger foothold in the UK, Europe, and Asia for high-impact research roles.

Website: nature.com/naturecareers

Mathematics

MathJobs.org

The absolute industry standard for mathematics. Most R1/R2 departments require applications to be submitted through this system. A math candidate not on this site is effectively invisible.

Website: mathjobs.org

Computer Science

CRA (Computing Research Association) Jobs

The premier board for tenure-track Computer Science research positions at top-tier North American institutions.

Website: cra.org/ads

ACM Career & Job Center

The Association for Computing Machinery board covers both academic faculty roles and high-level industrial research (e.g., Microsoft Research, Google).

Website: jobs.acm.org

Engineering

AcademicKeys (Engineering)

Deeply entrenched in the engineering academy; particularly useful for finding senior roles like Department Chairs and Deans of Engineering.

Website: engineering.academickeys.com

IEEE Job Site

While often industry-focused, the IEEE board is essential for Electrical and Computer Engineering faculty roles that require specific technical accreditations.

Website: jobs.ieee.org

Health & Medicine

AAMC CareerConnect

The Association of American Medical Colleges board is the standard for medical school faculty, including clinical tracks (MD) and basic science tracks (PhD).

Website: careerconnect.aamc.org

AACN Career Center (Nursing)

The American Association of Colleges of Nursing board is the primary hub for nursing faculty, Dean, and Director positions.

Website: aacnnursing.org/career-center

Economics

JOE Network (Job Openings for Economists)

Run by the American Economic Association. The entire economics hiring cycle is coordinated through this platform. It is mandatory for serious candidates.

Website: aeaweb.org/joe

EconJobMarket.org

A central platform for econometrics and international roles that operates alongside JOE, often used for centralized application collection.

Website: econjobmarket.org

INOMICS

Excellent for international (non-US) economics positions and conference announcements.

Website: inomics.com

Psychology

APA psycCareers

The American Psychological Association's board is the most robust source for both academic (teaching/research) and clinical-academic roles.

Website: jobs.psyccareers.com

Psych Job Wiki

A crowdsourced resource that tracks the real-time status of searches (interviews sent, offers made). Essential for monitoring the "pulse" of the market.

Website: psychjobwiki.org ( sites.google.com/view/psychjobwiki2425 )

History

H-Net Job Guide

The central nervous system for history and humanities recruitment. It provides the most detailed job descriptions and covers interdisciplinary fields.

Website: jobs.h-net.org

AHA Career Center

The American Historical Association board is essential for US-based tenure-track history roles.

Website: historians.org/jobs

English & Literature

MLA Job Information List

The Modern Language Association's list is the definitive source for English and Foreign Language jobs. Access often requires departmental membership.

Website: joblist.mla.org

UPenn Call for Papers (CFP)

While primarily for conferences, this site often lists fellowships and visiting positions in English and Literature not found elsewhere.

Website: call-for-papers.sas.upenn.edu

Philosophy & Theology

PhilJobs: Jobs for Philosophers

The global standard for philosophy hiring. Its integration with the PhilPapers bibliography database makes it unique.

Website: philjobs.org

AAR/SBL Employment Services

The primary market for Religious Studies and Theology, run by the American Academy of Religion and Society of Biblical Literature.

Website: aarsbl.org/job-listings-search

Arts, Music & Design

CAA (College Art Association) Careers

The main hub for art history, studio art, and design faculty positions.

Website: collegeart.org/jobs

Musical Chairs

A global board for performance and teaching roles in music conservatories and universities.

Website: musicalchairs.info

NYFA (New York Foundation for the Arts)

Excellent for practicing artists seeking "alt-ac," adjunct, or visiting artist roles.

Website: nyfa.org/jobs

Business & Management

Academy of Management (AOM) Career Center

The primary association board for tenure-track management, OB, and strategy roles.

Website: career-center.aom.org

Akadeus

Specializes in business school jobs globally, with a strong focus on the European and Asian business school markets.

Website: akadeus.com

Law

AALS Faculty Recruitment Services

The Association of American Law Schools manages the "Faculty Appointments Register" (FAR), the central channel for entry-level law professor hiring.

Website: aals.org/services/recruitment

SSRN (Social Science Research Network)

Used heavily for announcements of open law faculty positions and fellowships, often distributed via their newsletters.

Website: ssrn.com
